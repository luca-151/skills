# Pre-Launch Checklist — Feature Launch

The readiness check before a feature launch proceeds. Mid-tier scope: more thorough than
an improvement launch, lighter than a new-product launch. All sections must be complete
or explicitly decided before launch day.

This checklist is structural. Specific items will be populated as we run actual feature
launches and learn what has caused problems. Placeholder sections note where specifics
are needed.

---

## Launch narrative and positioning

- [ ] Launch narrative document written, reviewed, and locked
- [ ] If the feature changes what the product is understood to do: positioning has been
      revisited and messaging architecture updated accordingly
- [ ] The feature is described consistently and accurately across all channel assets
- [ ] No asset overstates what the feature does or implies capabilities not yet shipped

*[To be populated: specific messaging standards for feature announcements, including

---

## Asset readiness

- [ ] All in-scope channel assets final
- [ ] All assets reviewed by at least one person who did not write them
- [ ] Subject lines and headlines reviewed separately from body copy
- [ ] All links live and correct
- [ ] Product page update drafted, approved, and staged for launch day deployment
- [ ] Any visual assets (screenshots, video, GIFs) are current, accurate, and
      approved for external use

## Distribution readiness

- [ ] Subscriber segments identified and confirmed
- [ ] Email configuration confirmed (list, from name, reply-to, send time)
- [ ] Social accounts ready
- [ ] Scheduled sends confirmed if scheduling ahead
- [ ] Churned-user segment confirmed (this tier includes re-engagement)

## Product readiness

- [ ] Feature is live and stable in production (not just in staging)
- [ ] Product team has confirmed the announcement is accurate about what shipped
- [ ] Known edge cases or limitations have been reviewed: are any of them likely to
      be discovered by users immediately post-launch in a way that would embarrass
      the announcement?

*[To be populated: specific product stability criteria and sign-off process.]*

## Internal alignment

- [ ] Product team has reviewed and approved the announcement
- [ ] Anyone who might receive a user question knows the launch is coming and what
      the feature does
- [ ] Support channel is aware

## Contingency

- [ ] If a stability issue is discovered post-launch, who owns the decision to
      communicate with users about it?
- [ ] If a scheduled send fails, what is the fallback and who executes it?
- [ ] If user response surfaces a significant misunderstanding, what's the response plan?

## Go / No-Go

Final checkpoint. All sections above complete: **Go**.

Any section incomplete:
- Document what is incomplete and why
- Make an explicit decision: resolve before launch, or proceed with the gap noted
- If proceeding with a gap, note who made the call and what the acceptable risk is
