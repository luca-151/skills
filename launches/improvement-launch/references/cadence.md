# Cadence — Improvement Launch

The timing and sequencing framework for an improvement launch. This is the lightest
tier — compressed lead time, fewer assets, a narrower coordination window. The goal is
efficient execution without over-engineering a small announcement.

Lead time from decision to launch: two to four weeks is typical. Below four weeks,
quality of assets suffers. Above six weeks, an improvement announcement risks feeling
stale before it ships.

---

## Pre-launch sequencing

### T-14 (two weeks before launch)

- Launch narrative paragraph written and reviewed. One author, one reviewer. Done in
  a single session.
- Channel assets scoped. Confirm which channels are in scope for this improvement —
  not every improvement warrants every channel.
- Drafts assigned. Each channel asset has a named owner.

### T-7 (one week before launch)

- All channel assets drafted.
- Internal review complete. At minimum: marketing lead and product owner have read
  every asset.
- Message alignment confirmed across assets — the improvement is described consistently
  across email and social.
- Distribution lists confirmed. The right subscriber segments are identified.

### T-2 (two days before launch)

- All assets final. No substantive edits after this point.
- Assets scheduled or queued where the platform supports it.
- Team briefed. Anyone who might receive a user question knows the announcement is
  coming and what it says.

### T-1 (day before launch)

- Final read of all assets in context. Check for typos, broken links, and anything
  that reads differently at the end than it did in draft.
- Confirm scheduled sends and posts.
- Identify the single person responsible for launch-day monitoring.

---

## Launch day

An improvement launch does not have a choreographed minute-by-minute schedule. The
coordination question is sequencing across channels: email typically goes first; social
follows within the same morning window. Do not stagger channels across days — for an
improvement announcement, the news cycle is short.

- Email sends: first, within the morning window
- Social posts: same day, within two hours of email
- Monitor for user responses. An improvement announcement can surface bugs or edge
  cases — route those to product immediately.

---

## Post-launch follow-through

### T+1

- Check open rates and engagement across channels.
- Note any user responses that require a reply or flag for product.

### T+7

- Brief retrospective: did the announcement reach the right users? Were there user
  questions or confusion that point to a messaging gap? Capture findings for the
  next improvement launch.

There is no extended post-launch content cycle for improvement tier. The announcement
is the moment. If the improvement generates user stories or outcomes worth documenting,
those belong in a future feature or thought-leadership piece, not in an extended launch
follow-through.
