# New Product Launch

Orchestrates a full launch for a net-new product — the heaviest launch tier. For entirely
new products or sub-brands that require the full suite of channels and maximum coordination.

## When to invoke

- When launching an entirely new product or sub-brand
- When the launch requires the full suite of channels and maximum coordination
- When introducing a product that needs fresh positioning and messaging from scratch

## Launch narrative

The launch narrative is the strategic upstream document for a new-product launch. Every
channel asset — email, social, landing page, press, long-form — is derived from it. No
channel asset should be drafted until the launch narrative is written, reviewed, and
locked.

The launch narrative is not a messaging document, not a positioning statement, and not a
campaign brief. It is the connective story between what the product is (positioning) and
how every channel will tell that story (execution). It anchors the launch and answers
the three questions that a new product must answer before any audience will accept it:

**Why now**: what about this moment — cultural, technological, behavioral — makes this
product necessary or newly possible? The why-now is not a market-size argument. It is
an account of how the world has changed in a way that makes this product's moment real.
A product without a why-now is early or miscalibrated. A product with a strong why-now
is inevitable.

**Why us**: what does Every, and specifically the team that built this product, know or
have that makes us the right builder? Credibility claims here must be specific and
falsifiable. General claims about passion or mission are not credibility; they are
noise. The why-us should point to observable evidence: prior work, demonstrated
competence, the specific insight that led to the product.

**Why this**: why does this specific approach — this set of product decisions — solve the
problem better than alternatives? What have we chosen to do, and chosen not to do, and
why? The why-this is where product philosophy lives. It is the answer to "couldn't
someone else just build the same thing?" The answer is no, and the launch narrative
explains why.

For full construction methodology, see `references/launch-narrative.md`.
