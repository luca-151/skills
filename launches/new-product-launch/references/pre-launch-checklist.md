# Pre-Launch Checklist — New Product Launch

The readiness check before a new-product launch proceeds. Full scope: the most thorough
checklist in the launch tier system. All sections must be complete or explicitly decided
before the launch sequence begins.

This checklist is structural. Specific items will be populated as we run actual new-
product launches and learn what has caused problems. Placeholder sections note where
specifics are needed.

---

## Strategy and narrative

- [ ] Positioning confirmed and the relevant positioning skill is complete
- [ ] Launch narrative written, reviewed, and locked — the why-now, why-us, why-this
      are all answered in specific, falsifiable terms (see `references/launch-narrative.md`)
- [ ] Brand-voice confirmed and the relevant brand-voice skill is complete
- [ ] All channel assets can be traced back to the launch narrative — if any asset
      cannot, it has been revised or the narrative has been updated to resolve the gap

## Asset readiness

- [ ] All in-scope channel assets final
- [ ] All assets reviewed by at least one person who did not write them
- [ ] Landing page final, staged, and ready for launch-day deployment
- [ ] Product page final
- [ ] Long-form assets (byline, thought-leadership) final and placed or ready to place
- [ ] Press materials final: pitch, embargo release, product fact sheet
- [ ] All email variants final: subscriber, paid user, churned user, prospect
- [ ] Social suite final across all in-scope platforms
- [ ] All links live and correct
- [ ] All visual assets (screenshots, video, product demo) approved for external use

## Distribution and press readiness

- [ ] All social accounts ready and accessible

- [ ] Press outlet list finalized
- [ ] Press relationships confirmed: who is reaching out to which journalist?
- [ ] Embargo dates and terms confirmed with all embargo recipients
- [ ] Press embargo recipients have the embargo materials and have confirmed receipt

## Product readiness

- [ ] Product is live and stable in production (not staging)
- [ ] Product team has confirmed the launch narrative and all assets are accurate
- [ ] Onboarding or sign-up flow is tested and working
- [ ] Any known edge cases or limitations have been reviewed: are there any that would
      embarrass the announcement if discovered by a journalist or early user?
- [ ] Customer support or feedback channel is ready to receive new-product inquiries

## Contingency

      how to communicate to users?

- [ ] If a scheduled send fails, who owns recovery and what is the fallback?
- [ ] If user response surfaces a significant misunderstanding of the product,

## Go / No-Go

Final checkpoint. This decision is made at T-2, not on launch day. If a no-go
condition is discovered on launch morning, the cost of delay is much higher — which
means this decision must be made with enough lead time to delay gracefully if needed.

All sections above complete: **Go**.

Any section incomplete:
- Document what is incomplete and why
- Make an explicit decision: resolve before launch, or proceed with the gap noted
- If proceeding with a gap, note who made the call, what the acceptable risk is,
  and what contingency applies
