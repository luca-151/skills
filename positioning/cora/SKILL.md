# Cora Positioning

Positioning framework for Cora, Every's email product. Sourced July 2026 from the ruled
canon: the Cora V2 Brand Strategy Mini-Doc, the messaging spine, and Web Copy R002 —
one document, June–July 2026. Voice execution lives in `brand-voice/cora` (the canon
doc's personality, tone registers, and voice rules port there).

## When to invoke

- When defining or refining Cora's brand position
- When a strategy or craft skill needs Cora positioning context
- When launching a Cora product or feature
- When Cora must be distinguished from email clients or AI email features

## Purpose, north star, promise

- **Purpose:** Cora makes email easier.
- **North star:** Email that thinks like you.
- **Brand promise:** Cora reads every message, finds the few that need you, and handles
  the rest — so you can stop babysitting your inbox. The promise is not speed, not
  organization, not calm. It's that it's *done*.

## The spine

- **Essence:** Cora is the inbox you already have, run by hyper-competent, personalized
  AI, built for where the future of work is going.
- **Lead line / hero: "Stop working for your inbox."**
- **Descriptor:** The AI inbox that does email for you.
- **Campaign provocation:** Don't do your email. (Deploy, not promise.)
- **Positioning statement:** Cora is AI for email. For people who lose hours a day to
  email, Cora reads every message, drafts the replies it can, and gives you a 30-second
  brief of the rest — so the only email left is the email that's actually for you.
  Where Superhuman makes you faster at email, Cora makes email need less of you.
- **The wedge (vs. the benchmark):** You don't need email with superpowers. You need
  your inbox to leave you alone.

Known surface tension (July 2026): Web Copy R002's hero reads "AI Native Email." while
the ruled spine's lead line is "Stop working for your inbox." — check the current copy
round before locking any hero surface. "Serious Email for Serious People" is the proof
bar hed, not the hero.

## The three pillars

| Pillar | One line | Carries |
|---|---|---|
| **Familiar** | It's still your inbox. | Kills adoption friction; same Gmail, no migration, nothing sent without you. |
| **Does the work** | You stop working for your email. | The core benefit and the labor flip: screens, drafts in your voice, briefs twice daily, learns who matters. |
| **Open** | It answers to you, not a platform. | The forward claim: best models from Google/Anthropic/OpenAI; web, iOS, Android, iMessage, CLI. |

## Enemy

The inbox as boss — the customer feels like they *work for* their email. Inbox zero is
a false god: even achieved, it resets by morning. Competitors make you faster at the
thing consuming you; speed is the trap (Superhuman's own churn proves it — users get
faster and don't spend less time). Never a named-competitor enemy on customer surfaces.

## Audience

**Category:** AI email management. **Target:** knowledge workers at 100+ emails/day who
want the inbox *dealt with*, not organized. Three researched personas (645 survey
responses + Kieran's persona research):

- **T1 Taylor — the overwhelmed executive** (150–300+/day, tried everything). Pain:
  "email is a full-time job I didn't sign up for." Way in: delegation — "Hand it off
  like you hand off your calendar." Churns if an important email is missed.
- **T2 Jordan — the anxious perfectionist** (60–150/day, tens of thousands of unreads).
  Pain: the one that gets away. Way in: Fresh Start + All Caught Up transparency —
  "Nothing slips, and you see everything it touched." Churns on ambiguity.
- **T3 Sam — the behavioral optimizer** (40–100/day, philosophically done with email).
  Way in: the ritual — "Two checks a day. That's the whole relationship." Churns if the
  ritual breaks or demands tinkering.

**Not our user:** email-obsessive power users (want control, not delegation), email
ignorers, low-volume (<15/day), and stay-in-Gmail users. Shared truth across all three
targets: trust is fragile — one missed email and they leave; when they believe, they
proselytize.

## Archetype

**Sage primary, Caregiver secondary.** The Sage grounds competence and trust — Cora
earns authority through results, delivering *understanding*. The Caregiver supplies the
emotional register — relief, service, anticipation. The tension held on purpose:
intelligent enough to trust, personal enough to feel of service. **Intelligence is the
mechanism. Relief is the outcome.** Personality expression (concierge tonality; Attuned,
Decisive, Forthright, Professional, Terse, Grounded) executes in `brand-voice/cora`.

## Positioning-relevant voice rules (full set in brand-voice/cora)

No speed language — speed is competitive territory and the wrong frame. No productivity
guilt — the customer already knows email hurts. "AI-powered" isn't enough — get
specific. Numbers over adjectives ("I handled 37 emails" beats "we managed your inbox").

## Proof points (sourced — cite when used)

275 interruptions/day per knowledge worker (Microsoft Work Trend Index 2025, n=31,000);
85% of emails read in under 15 seconds (MS WTI 2024); 78.7% of workers have felt inbox
dread (EmailTooltester 2024, n=1,125); 23m15s to refocus after an interruption (Gloria
Mark, UC Irvine); Superhuman churn: "faster at email but not spending less time"
(alfred churn analysis, Mar 2026). Product proof: agent-native architecture, the Brief,
Focus Mode, All Caught Up's transparent handled count, fidelity levels, everywhere
(web/iOS/Android/iMessage/CLI). Pedigree proof: built by Every; Kieran's standing in
the community sets a built-in audience with high expectations.
