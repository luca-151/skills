# Research

How we conduct and synthesize marketing research at Every. This skill covers the full arc
from discovery through synthesis — interviews, competitive audits, category landscapes,
and the methodology that turns raw findings into strategic inputs.

Loaded by positioning, archetyping, and any skill doing discovery work. Research is
upstream of almost everything: no positioning framework, audience archetype, or messaging
architecture should be built without it.

## System

Research is composed of five reference files covering the research lifecycle:

### references/discovery-interviews.md
How we design and conduct discovery interviews — stakeholder, customer, and prospect.
The qualitative foundation for positioning and voice work.

### references/competitive-audits.md
How we audit competitors — what we look at, how we structure findings, and how audits
feed into positioning and messaging.

### references/category-landscape.md
How we map the category a brand operates in — market structure, adjacent categories,
whitespace, and category dynamics over time.

### references/synthesis-methodology.md
How we synthesize research into usable strategic inputs — the bridge between raw
findings and the frameworks that positioning and archetyping skills consume.

### references/research-briefs.md
How we write research briefs — the document that scopes a research effort before it
begins and ensures alignment on objectives, methods, and deliverables.

## When to invoke

- When a positioning skill needs audience or competitive context
- When scoping a new market or evaluating a competitive landscape
- When conducting discovery interviews for a new or existing brand
- When synthesizing findings from multiple research inputs into strategic recommendations
- When writing a research brief to scope an upcoming research effort
- When validating assumptions before a brand, product, or campaign decision
