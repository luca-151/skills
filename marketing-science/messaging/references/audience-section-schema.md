# Audience-Section Schema

The canonical format for messaging documents. This is the per-target section structure every
audience-facing messaging doc uses, plus the document-level framing that opens it and the
evidence ledger that closes each section.

Source: Every Agent messaging framework session, July 13, 2026 (product referred to as
"@Every" / "the Every Agent," formerly Plus One).

Cross-reference: this operationalizes the message house and audience-proposition matrix in
`strategy/messaging-architecture` at the section level — it does not replace them. The
sourcing and verification rules each field must satisfy live in
`craft/copywriting/references/messaging-doc-discipline.md`.

---

## Per target, in order

- **WHO** — one line.
- **PAIN** — evidenced, named studies inline.
- **MOTIVATION** — [H] until validated.
- **HOW WE SOLVE IT** — mechanism plus 2–3 sample lines cascaded from pillars.
- **THE COMPETITION** — verbatim quotes per named competitor, dated.
- **WHY US** — the differentiated answer, one or two sentences, absence claims dated.
- **WHERE** — researched channel evidence; published sources on where this role congregates and consumes.

## Document framing

- **Open the doc** with the macro backdrop: the category-level data that frames why the timing works, cited.
- **Close each section** with a one-line evidence ledger: what's verified, what's hypothesis, what's pending re-check.
